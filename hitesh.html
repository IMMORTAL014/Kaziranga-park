About Kaziranga

A Sanctuary of Extraordinary Biodiversity

Kaziranga National Park, located in the Golaghat and Nagaon districts of Assam, India, is a World Heritage Site celebrated for providing critical and final habitat for the endangered great one-horned rhinoceros.

Established in 1908, the park spans an area of 430 square kilometers and is characterized by tall elephant grass, dense tropical moist broadleaf forests, pure stands of canebrakes, and numerous swampy lagoons.

Recognized as an Important Bird Area by BirdLife International, Kaziranga hosts significant breeding populations of the critically endangered Bengal florican, as well as the only known population of the wild water buffalo in the world.

Iconic Wildlife

Great One-Horned Rhinoceros

Flagship Species

Kaziranga is home to over 2,400 one-horned rhinoceroses, representing about two-thirds of the world's total population of this magnificent species.

Royal Bengal Tiger

Apex Predator

With one of the highest density of tigers among protected areas in the world, Kaziranga is an important Tiger Reserve with approximately 100 tigers.

Wild Water Buffalo

Endangered

Kaziranga hosts the largest population of wild water buffalo in the world, with over 3,500 individuals representing about 57% of the global population.
